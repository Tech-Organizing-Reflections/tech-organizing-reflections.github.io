Call for Participation

We welcome submissions from both academics and tech worker organizers. Submissions may take the form of a 2-3 page position paper, in ACM format, or 250 words about yourself and your interest in this workshop. If you are submitting a position paper, please indicate whether you are interested in publishing this work via the Tech Otherwise site.

This workshop is hybrid, and open to attendees from beyond the Aarhus conference. Please notify us via THIS RSVP form if you plan to attend online or in-person.

Submissions will be accepted until July 21, and we will notify participants of acceptance by July 28, three weeks in advance of the workshop. Online attendance will be capped at 50 participants to enable meaningful conversation and discussion across the group.

Themes

We invite papers that reflect on contemporary practice and histories of activism. We are especially interested in reflections on what strategies worked and did not work. Papers can reflect, but are not limited to:

  • Reflections on contemporary struggles

    Reflections on the last decade of collective resistance and worker organizing, including popular worker-led responses to the tech sector and the subsequent corporate responseses

  • Reflections on interventions by critical computing scholars

    Reflections on scholarly interventions on the tech sector and how that has shaped the current techno-fascist moment

  • Reflections on previous movement histories

    Reflections on longer history of collective organizing, both in computing and outside of it, and evaluation of the potential lessons and strategic insights from prior decades of resistance
